# European Studies Self-Study Roadmap with Resources

## Intro European Studies

| Subject | Why Study? | Book | Online Course |
|---------|------------|------------------|---------------|
| Introduction to European Studies | Provides an interdisciplinary overview of Europe's geography, diversity, identities, and the concept of "Europe" across history and today. | [*Europe: A History* by Norman Davies](https://ia801405.us.archive.org/30/items/davies-norman-europe-a-history-1997/Davies%2C%20Norman%20-%20Europe%20-%20A%20History%20%281997%29.pdf) | [European Culture and Politics (FutureLearn)](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/european-culture) |
| European Geography and Societies | Understands Europe's physical and human diversity, including regional differences, migration, and social structures. |  | [Geography Now! Europe Series (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLR7XO54Pktt-h8T-dtr4MXy0_MpbWukHW) |
| Basic European Language Study | Builds foundational skills for accessing original sources and understanding cultural nuances. |  |  |

## Core European Studies

| Subject | Why Study? | Book | Online Course |
|---------|------------|------------------|---------------|
| European History: Early Modern to 19th Century | Traces the foundations of modern Europe through Renaissance, Reformation, Enlightenment, revolutions, and nation-building. | [*A History of Modern Europe* by John Merriman](https://www.amazon.com/History-Modern-Europe-Renaissance-Present/dp/0393934330) | [European Civilization, 1648-1945 (Yale Open Courses)](https://oyc.yale.edu/history/hist-202) |
| European History: 20th–21st Century | Examines wars, totalitarianism, Cold War division, postwar reconstruction, and contemporary challenges. | [*Postwar: A History of Europe Since 1945* by Tony Judt](https://www.amazon.com/Postwar-History-Europe-Since-1945/dp/0143037757) | Covered in European Civilization, 1648-1945 (Yale Open Courses) |
| Introduction to European Politics and Governments | Covers political systems, comparative politics in Europe, and the evolution of democracy and authoritarianism. | [*European Politics: A Comparative Introduction* by Tim Bale](https://www.amazon.com/European-Politics-Comparative-Introduction-Bale/dp/1137581336) | [European Culture and Politics (FutureLearn)](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/european-culture) |
| History and Politics of the European Union | Explores the origins, integration process, institutions, and key policies of the EU. | [*The European Union: Politics and Policies* by Jonathan Olsen and John McCormick](https://www.amazon.com/European-Union-Politics-Policies/dp/0813348986) | [EU Policy and Implementation: Making Europe Work (Coursera)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/eu-making-europe-work) |
| European Economies and the Single Market | Understands economic integration, the eurozone, and Europe's role in global economics. | [*The Economics of European Integration* by Richard Baldwin and Charles Wyplosz](https://www.amazon.com/Economics-European-Integration-Richard-Baldwin/dp/1526847213) | [The Eurozone Crisis (Marginal Revolution University)](https://mru.org/eurozone-crisis) |
| European Cultures and Identities | Analyzes literature, art, philosophy, and cultural movements shaping European identity and diversity. |  |  |
| Continued Language Study | Advances to intermediate proficiency for reading texts and media. |  |  |

## Advanced European Studies

### History and Culture

| Subject | Why Study? | Book | Online Course | Prerequisites |
|---------|------------|------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Medieval and Renaissance Europe | Explores foundations of European culture, religion, and society. | [*The Making of Europe* by Robert Bartlett](https://www.amazon.com/Making-Europe-Conquest-Colonization-950-1350/dp/0691037809) | [The Early Middle Ages, 284–1000 (Yale Open Courses)](https://oyc.yale.edu/history/hist-210) | Core History |
| Modern European Intellectual History | Examines key thinkers, philosophies, and ideas shaping Europe. |  | Covered in European Civilization, 1648-1945 (Yale Open Courses) | Core History |
| Contemporary European Society and Migration | Analyzes postwar social changes, multiculturalism, and identity crises. | *The Strange Death of Europe* by Douglas Murray |  | Core phase |
| European Art and Literature | Studies major movements and works reflecting European experience. | [*Ways of Seeing* by John Berger](https://www.amazon.com/Ways-Seeing-John-Berger/dp/0140135154) |  | Cultures subject |

### Politics and the European Union

| Subject | Why Study? | Book | Online Course | Prerequisites |
|---------|------------|------------------|---------------|---------------|
| EU Institutions and Decision-Making | Deepens knowledge of how the EU functions internally. | *Understanding the European Union* by John McCormick | Covered in EU Policy and Implementation: Making Europe Work (Coursera) | EU Politics |
| EU Policies (e.g., Foreign, Security, Enlargement) | Examines external relations and policy challenges. | *The Foreign Policy of the European Union* by Federiga Bindi |  | EU Politics |
| Comparative European Politics | Compares national systems and EU influence. | [*Foundations of European Politics* by Catherine de Vries et al.](https://www.amazon.com/Foundations-European-Politics-Comparative-Approach/dp/0198831307) |  | European Politics |
| Europe in Global Affairs | Positions Europe in international relations and geopolitics. | [*The European Union as a Global Actor* by Charlotte Bretherton and John Vogler](https://www.amazon.com/European-Union-Global-Actor/dp/0415282454) |  | Core Politics |

### Economics and Society

| Subject | Why Study? | Book | Online Course | Prerequisites |

| European Social Models and Welfare States | Compares welfare systems and social policies. | [*The New Old World* by Perry Anderson](https://www.amazon.com/New-Old-World-Perry-Anderson/dp/1844677214) |  | Core phase |

| Advanced Language Study | Reaches advanced proficiency for specialized texts. |  |  | Intermediate Language |
